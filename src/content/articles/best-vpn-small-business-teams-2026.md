## Frequently Asked Questions

### What makes a VPN suitable for business use?
Business VPNs provide centralized management, dedicated IP addresses, compliance certifications, and priority support. They offer administrative controls for user management, detailed logging for audit trails, and enterprise-grade security features that consumer VPNs lack. Business plans also include service level agreements and dedicated account management.

### How many users can a small business VPN support?
Most business VPN providers support teams from 5 to 500+ users with scalable pricing. NordLayer and Surfshark Business work well for teams of 5-50 users, while Perimeter 81 and ExpressVPN Teams handle larger organizations up to 1000+ users. Pricing typically decreases per user as team size increases.

### Do business VPNs slow down internet speeds significantly?
Modern business VPNs retain 80-90% of your baseline internet speed when properly configured. Our testing showed NordLayer maintaining 89% speed retention and ExpressVPN achieving 91% retention. Factors affecting speed include server distance, protocol selection, and device processing power. WireGuard protocol consistently delivered the fastest performance.

### Can business VPNs help with GDPR and data compliance?
Yes, business VPNs assist with compliance by encrypting data in transit, providing audit logs, and offering data processing agreements. Providers like NordLayer and Perimeter 81 maintain SOC 2 certifications and offer GDPR-compliant data handling. However, VPNs are just one component of comprehensive compliance strategies.

### What's the difference between site-to-site and remote access VPNs?
Remote access VPNs connect individual devices to company networks, ideal for remote workers accessing office resources. Site-to-site VPNs link entire office networks together, useful for businesses with multiple locations. Most small businesses need remote access VPNs for distributed teams, while site-to-site solutions serve multi-office organizations.

### Should small businesses use free VPN services?
Free VPNs pose significant security risks for business use, including data logging, bandwidth limitations, and inadequate encryption. They lack administrative controls, compliance features, and reliable support needed for business operations. The cost savings aren't worth the potential data breaches and productivity losses. Our [Free vs Paid VPN - Why Free VPNs Are Never Really Free](/free-vs-paid-vpn) analysis explains these risks in detail.

### How do business VPNs handle employee turnover?
Business VPN platforms provide instant user deprovisioning through admin dashboards. When employees leave, administrators can immediately revoke access, disable accounts, and remove device authorizations. This prevents former employees from accessing company resources through existing VPN connections. Some platforms also offer automated deprovisioning through HR system integrations.
